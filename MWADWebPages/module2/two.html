For Module 2, I based design elements off of what I found on www.kwaytrucking.com. The kwaytrucking site is far and beyond more advanced than this current page, but the basic design elements are there. Navigation bar is stuck to the top, logo in the top left, and bodies of text are centered in column making the page responsive. The color scheme using blue and green contrasting colors seems to work well. The logo was created in draw.io and 3DPaint.

This site will serve as my central hub for the rest of the class. The website that is developed for module 3 will link into the module 3 tab, module 4 will link into the module 4 tab, and so on so forth.

Here's a picture a jet that I helped transport from a port to a defense contractor that trains air-force pilots.

Helping transport jets was absolutely one of the highlights of my career as a freight broker. I did have the benefit booking business opportunitites for many defense contractor loads. Another cool opportunity I had the honor in helping with was bringing a prototype CV90 MKIV to a tradeshow from the port. This was indeed another one of the coolest opportunties for several reasons. First, here's a CV90 MK IV:

What made moving this tank a logistics opportunity of a lifetime was the fact that it was picked up from the airport off of one of the Antonov's. The Antonov is a series of the largest cargo jets in the world. The Antonov is an aircraft fleet owned by Ukraine which can be chartered by anyone that has a few hundred thousand dollars to ship a large heavy object as quickly as possible.

The AN-124 is the second largest Antonov jet in the fleet, behind the AN-225. It was the largest jet in the world for 30 years straight. The AN-225 is now the largest jet in operation.
